Red Flags on Public Wi-Fi Networks

No password required:

  • If the Wi-Fi is completely open (no password), it’s usually unencrypted, meaning your data can be intercepted.
Suspicious network names:
  • If you see multiple networks with similar names (e.g., “Airport_Wi-Fi” and “Free_Airport_Wi-Fi”), one could be a fake hotspot.
No HTTPS padlock:
  • When browsing, if websites don’t show the padlock in the address bar, your connection isn’t secure.
Pop-ups or redirects:
  • If connecting to Wi-Fi forces you through strange login pages or redirects you to odd websites, that’s a red flag.
Excessive permissions:
  • Some public Wi-Fi portals ask for personal info (like email or phone number) before granting access—this can be a phishing tactic.
