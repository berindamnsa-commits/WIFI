WHAT MAKES PUBLIC WI-FI NETWORKS SO RISKY DESPITE ITS BENEFITS

Data Interception

  • > How it happens: When you connect to public Wi-Fi, your data travels through the airwaves. If the network isn’t encrypted, hackers can use tools called “packet sniffers” to capture the information being transmitted.
  • > What they can see: Login credentials, emails, credit card numbers, or even the websites you visit.
  • > Real-world example: Imagine logging into your bank account at a café. A hacker nearby could intercept your username and password if the connection isn’t secure.
Fake Hotspots (Rogue Access Points)
  • > How it happens: Cybercriminals set up a Wi-Fi network with a name that looks legitimate, like “Free_Airport_Wi-Fi” or “Starbucks_Wi-Fi.”
  • > Why it’s dangerous: Once you connect, all your traffic flows through their system. They can monitor everything you do, inject malware, or redirect you to fake websites.
  • > Analogy: It’s like getting into a taxi that looks official but is actually run by a scammer who takes you to the wrong place.
Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks
  • > How it happens: A hacker positions themselves between you and the website or service you’re trying to access. They intercept and possibly alter the communication without you knowing.
  • > Impact: Sensitive information like login credentials, messages, or financial transactions can be stolen or manipulated.
  • > Analogy: It’s like whispering a secret to a friend, but someone else is sitting between you two, listening and changing the message before it reaches your friend.
Malware Distribution
  • > How it happens: Hackers can exploit unsecured public Wi-Fi to push malicious software onto connected devices.
  • > Impact: Once infected, your device could be used to steal data, spy on your activity, or even become part of a botnet.
Unencrypted Connections
  • > What encryption does: Encryption scrambles your data so only the intended recipient can read it.
  • > The risk: Many public Wi-Fi networks don’t use encryption (or use outdated methods like WEP). This means anyone else on the same network can potentially see your activity.
  • > Impact: Even something as simple as browsing social media could expose your login session to attackers.
DANGERS IN PUBLIC WI-FI

DANGERS IN USING PUBLIC WI-FI

What is Public Wi-Fi?

Public Wi-Fi networks are shared internet connections available in public places such as airports, coffee shops, hotels, libraries, and malls. Anyone nearby can connect, usually without a private password.

Why People Use Public Wi-Fi

Convenience

  • > Instant access: You don’t need to set up anything—just connect and go. Perfect for travelers who don’t want to fiddle with SIM cards or roaming plans.
  • > Remote work:Digital nomads, students, and professionals can quickly check emails, join meetings, or upload files from almost anywhere.
  • > No tethering required:Instead of draining your phone battery by using it as a hotspot, you can rely on public Wi-Fi.
Cost
  • > Free service: Most public Wi-Fi hotspots are offered at no charge, making them attractive for people who want to avoid expensive mobile data plans
  • >Data savings: Streaming videos, downloading large files, or updating apps can eat up mobile data quickly. Public Wi-Fi lets you do these tasks without worrying about overage fees.
  • > Budget-friendly travel: Avoid roaming charges. Tourists often rely on public Wi-Fi to navigate maps, translate languages, or book tickets without incurring roaming charges.
Ubiquity
  • > Widespread availability: Found almost everywhere. From airports and hotels to coffee shops and even buses
  • > Always connected: Businesses offer Wi-Fi as standard as part of their customer service
  • > Modern lifestyle support: Keeps people connected on the go (social media, work platforms, and entertainment).
