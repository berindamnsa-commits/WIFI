DANGERS IN USING PUBLIC WI-FI

What is Public Wi-Fi?

Public Wi-Fi networks are shared internet connections available in public places such as airports, coffee shops, hotels, libraries, and malls. Anyone nearby can connect, usually without a private password.

Why People Use Public Wi-Fi

Convenience

  • > Instant access: You don’t need to set up anything—just connect and go. Perfect for travelers who don’t want to fiddle with SIM cards or roaming plans.
  • > Remote work:Digital nomads, students, and professionals can quickly check emails, join meetings, or upload files from almost anywhere.
  • > No tethering required:Instead of draining your phone battery by using it as a hotspot, you can rely on public
Cost
  • > Free service: Most public Wi-Fi hotspots are offered at no charge, making them attractive for people who want to avoid expensive mobile data plans
  • >Data savings: Streaming videos, downloading large files, or updating apps can eat up mobile data quickly. Public Wi-Fi lets you do these tasks without worrying about overage fees.
  • > Budget-friendly travel: Avoid roaming charges. Tourists often rely on public Wi-Fi to navigate maps, translate languages, or book tickets without incurring roaming charges.
Ubiquity
  • > Widespread availability: Found almost everywhere.From airports and hotels to coffee shops and even buses
  • > Always connected: Businesses offer Wi-Fi as standard as part of their customer service
  • > Modern lifestyle support: Keeps people connected on the go (social media, work platforms, and entertainment).
